## Below are 30 practice questions for the HR Employee Dataset (hr\_employee\_500.csv).





Divided into Easy (10), Medium (10), and Hard (10).



### **EASY LEVEL (Exploration + Basic Filtering)**



1. How many employees are there in each department?
   
2. What is the gender distribution in the company (percentage)?
   
3. Find the average salary of the company.
   
4. List employees who are older than 50 years.
   
5. Find employees with performance rating = 5.
   
6. Count how many employees joined after 2020.
   
7. What is the youngest and oldest employee age?
   
8. Display top 10 highest-paid employees.
   
9. How many employees are there in each designation?
   
10. Find average experience of employees in the IT department.



### **MEDIUM LEVEL (Business Logic + Grouping)**



1. Find department-wise average salary.
   
2. Which department has the highest average performance rating?
   
3. Identify employees whose salary is above company average.
   
4. Create an Age Group column:

   20–30 → Young

   31–45 → Mid

   46+ → Senior
   Then count employees per age group.
   
5. Find employees whose experience is greater than department average experience.
   
6. Calculate salary growth ratio:
   Salary / Experience\_Years
   Find top 10 employees with highest ratio.
   
7. Which designation has highest average salary?
   
8. Find departments where average salary > 80,000.
   
9. Calculate total salary cost per department.
   
10. Identify employees who joined in the same year but different departments.



### **HARD LEVEL (Advanced Analysis + HR Insights)**



1. Find employees whose salary is above department average but performance rating below department average.
   
2. Detect salary outliers in each department using IQR method.
   
3. Create promotion risk flag:

   Experience > 8 years

   Performance rating >= 4

   Salary below department median
   Identify such employees.
   
4. Find departments where average experience is higher than company-wide average experience.
   
5. Calculate correlation between Age, Salary, Experience, and Performance\_Rating.
   
6. Identify high-risk attrition candidates:

   Age < 30

   Salary below company median

   Performance rating <= 2
   
7. Create a ranking of employees within each department based on salary.
   
8. Find salary inequality:
   Calculate difference between highest and lowest salary in each department.
   
9. Perform cohort analysis:
   Group employees by joining year and calculate average salary per cohort.
   
10. Create 3 employee segments based on performance:

    Low (1–2)

    Medium (3–4)

    High (5)

&nbsp;  Compare average salary and experience across segments.
